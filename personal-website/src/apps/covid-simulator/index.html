Why is it important?

We believe showcasing a simulation would be valuable to teach the importance of social distancing.

What can I do with it?

With this site, you can see the effects of how quick or slow the virus spreads depending on differing enviromental conditions and the social distancing measures taken.

Who is it for?

Governemental agencies and technical advisors. It is paramount that our leaders understand the impact of social distancing as broadly as possible. With this simulation, we hope to further this understanding.

How does it work?


The page has an enviroment with lots of bubbles that represent individuals. Red bubbles are infected, blue are healthy, grey are immune.


Some bubbles will be immobile and some move around. If a red (sick) bubble bumps into a blue (healthy) bubble, it will turn red. After a short time, the red bubbles will turn grey (immune/recovered).


You have the ability to control the size of the environment, the size of the bubbles, the number of bubbles (up to 500) and which ones stay still, the initial ratio of sick to healthy, and the speed of the bubbles.
